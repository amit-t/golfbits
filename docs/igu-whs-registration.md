# Amit's 2026 walkthrough: get an official IGU/WHS Handicap Index

_Last checked: 7 July 2026. Use this as a practical checklist, not legal/tournament advice. Re-verify all fees, portal labels, upload requirements, and BMW Golf Cup entry rules on the live IGU WHS and BMW pages before paying or registering._

## Why this matters for Amit

You are a Bangalore-based, right-handed beginner golfer, roughly a 110 shooter, with Clover Greens (Hosur, near Bangalore) as your regular course. Your goal is not to look fancy on WhatsApp. It is to get a real, portable **IGU/WHS Handicap Index** that tournament desks accept, then work it down toward **BMW Golf Cup Category B**, which BMW India lists for 2026 as **handicaps 13-28**.

The shortest path:

1. Register as an **IGU WHS Non-Club Member**.
2. Post acceptable scores totalling **54 holes**.
3. Keep posting every eligible round the same day.
4. Watch the index become more stable once you have 20 score differentials.
5. Target **28.0 or lower** for Category B eligibility, then keep improving.

## Part 2 — Post scores correctly

### 1. Know the 54-hole requirement

To establish an initial Handicap Index, WHS requires scores totalling **54 holes**. Practically, that can be:

- Three 18-hole rounds, or
- Six 9-hole rounds, or
- Any valid 9-hole/18-hole mix that totals 54 holes, subject to what IGU's portal accepts.

For Amit at Clover Greens, easiest path:

1. Play 18 holes at Clover Greens.
2. Post the score the same day.
3. Repeat twice more.
4. After 54 posted holes, check whether the portal has issued your initial Handicap Index.

**Re-verify on live portal:** IGU, as the national authority, controls the exact Indian implementation and form choices for initial score submission.

### 2. Use Clover Greens confidently, but verify tee rating on the day

Clover Greens Golf Course and Resort is a real 18-hole course at Hosur, near Bangalore. Public scorecard/rating listings show rated tees such as:

- Black: 6,950 yards, Course Rating 70.6, Slope 122
- Blue: 6,465 yards, Course Rating 67.5, Slope 118
- White: 5,785 yards, Course Rating 64.5, Slope 110

For a ~110 shooter, do not let ego pick the tee. Start from the tee Clover Greens staff/pro recommend for your current ability. White or another forward tee may produce better pace, better learning, and cleaner handicap data.

Before each posted round, confirm:

- Tee colour used
- Course Rating
- Slope Rating
- Par
- Stroke index/hole handicap values

These are usually printed on the scorecard or available from the club/pro shop. If the IGU WHS portal has Clover Greens in its course database, select the matching course and tee instead of manually guessing.

**Re-verify on live portal and at Clover Greens:** course ratings can change after rerating, redesign, tee changes, or database updates. Clover Greens has also publicised a par-72 championship-layout transformation, so use the current IGU/club rating, not an old internet scorecard.

### 3. Play with a marker and keep an attested card

For handicap purposes, do not post solo fantasy golf. Play with at least one other person who can verify your score.

After the round:

1. Compare hole-by-hole scores with your playing partner/marker.
2. Resolve penalties and pickups before leaving the scoring area.
3. Have the playing partner/marker sign or attest the card.
4. Sign it yourself.
5. Photograph both sides of the card before it gets sweaty, folded, or lost in the car.

Use attested scorecards even if the portal only asks for manual entry. It protects you if IGU, a tournament desk, or a handicap review asks for support later.

**Re-verify on live portal:** IGU's current portal may require manual entry, hole-by-hole entry, adjusted gross entry, scorecard upload, marker details, or a different attestation workflow.

### 4. Post the score the same day

WHS expects scores to be submitted as soon as possible on the day of play and before midnight local time. Same-day posting matters because:

- Your Handicap Index can update for the next day.
- The score can be included in Playing Conditions Calculation (PCC), if applicable.
- Your scoring record stays chronological and defensible.

For Amit's routine:

1. Finish round at Clover Greens.
2. Sit down with water/lime soda before driving back to Bangalore.
3. Open the IGU WHS portal.
4. Enter the score before leaving the club, or at least before midnight India time.
5. Save confirmation/screenshot.

If the portal allows hole-by-hole posting, use it. It reduces net-double-bogey mistakes and preserves cleaner data.

### 5. Apply the maximum hole score correctly

For handicap posting, one disaster hole should not wreck the entire differential.

Important distinction:

- **Before you have an initial Handicap Index:** the USGA WHS guidance says the maximum hole score for initial scores is **par + 5**.
- **After your Handicap Index is established:** the usual maximum is **net double bogey**.

Net double bogey means:

> Par + 2 + any handicap strokes you receive on that hole based on your Course Handicap.

Example after Amit has an index:

- Hole: par 4
- Amit receives 1 stroke on that hole
- Net double bogey max: 4 + 2 + 1 = **7**
- If he actually makes 9 after bunker-water-three-putt theatre, he posts **7** for handicap purposes.

If you enter hole-by-hole in the portal, the system may apply this automatically. If you enter an adjusted gross score, you must adjust blow-up holes yourself.

**Re-verify in the IGU portal:** use the portal's own instructions for initial-score maximums, hole-by-hole entry, and adjusted gross entry. If the portal applies net double bogey automatically, do not double-adjust.

## Part 3 — Understand how the index is computed and what milestones matter

### 1. The score differential is the real unit

Your Handicap Index is not a simple average of gross scores. Each round becomes a **Score Differential**, which adjusts for course difficulty.

Standard 18-hole formula:

```text
Score Differential = (113 / Slope Rating) × (Adjusted Gross Score - Course Rating - PCC adjustment)
```

Meaning:

- Same 100 at a harder course can be better than 100 at an easier course.
- Same 100 from different tees at Clover Greens can produce different differentials.
- Posting the right tee is not clerical fuss. It changes the math.

### 2. Initial index milestone: 54 holes posted

Your first major milestone is not breaking 100. It is:

> **54 valid holes posted and accepted in the IGU WHS portal.**

Once that happens, the system should issue an initial Handicap Index. It may be high. Fine. A 110 shooter may start somewhere in the 30s or 40s depending on tee, rating, slope, and adjusted hole scores. Your job is to make it official first, then make it lower.

Checklist for this milestone:

- [ ] IGU WHS account active
- [ ] 18-hole score 1 posted
- [ ] 18-hole score 2 posted
- [ ] 18-hole score 3 posted
- [ ] All cards attested and photographed
- [ ] Initial Handicap Index visible in portal
- [ ] Handicap certificate/download found, if portal provides one

### 3. Stable-index milestone: 20 score differentials

Once you have 20 scores/differentials in the record, WHS uses the **best 8 of your most recent 20**. The other 12 are ignored for that calculation.

This is good news for a beginner:

- One awful monsoon round does not define you.
- Improvement shows up when your better differentials replace old weak ones.
- Consistent bogey/double-bogey golf matters more than one heroic par followed by three lost balls.

Keep posting every acceptable round. The system needs data.

### 4. BMW Golf Cup Category B milestone: 28.0 or lower

BMW Group India's 2026 press release lists two India categories:

- Category A: handicaps up to 12
- **Category B: handicaps 13-28**

For Amit, practical target:

> Get an official IGU/WHS Handicap Index, then bring it to **28.0 or lower** before trying to enter Category B.

Also note:

- BMW rules can require owner/customer eligibility and supporting documents.
- BMW may decide final participation confirmation.
- Local event dates and venues can change.
- For 2026, BMW listed Bengaluru qualifiers at Prestige Golfshire on 11 and 12 March 2026; future editions may differ.

**Re-verify before entering:** Category limits, required handicap proof, BMW owner/customer rules, city schedule, and accepted certificate format on the live BMW Golf Cup page/invitation.
