# Amit's Golf Playbook
*Bangalore · Right-handed · ~110 shooter · Budget ₹80k–1.5L · Goal: respected amateur with a real handicap*

---

## 1. The Bag That Belongs

**The truth first:** at elite clubs, nobody judges *which* brand you play. They judge whether your gear looks *chosen* — a composed set, clean, with headcovers — versus a boxed "package set" or rusty mismatched starters. A mid-range fitted set beats an expensive random one.

### What to buy (₹80k–1.5L, right-handed)

| Slot | Recommendation | ~Price |
|---|---|---|
| **Irons 6–PW (graphite or light steel, Regular flex)** | Callaway Elyte X, TaylorMade Qi HL, or Ping G440 — new. Or one gen old (Rogue ST Max, Stealth HD, G425) pre-owned to save ₹25k | ₹55–75k new / ₹35–45k used |
| **Driver (10.5°, Regular)** | One-gen-old premium pre-owned: Qi10 Max, Paradym X, Ping G430 Max | ₹25–35k used |
| **Hybrids 4H + 5H** (skip long irons entirely — you're a 110 shooter, they'll punish you) | Same family as irons, or Cobra | ₹12–18k each |
| **Wedges** | Set PW + a 56° sand wedge (Callaway CB / Cleveland CBX — cavity back, not blade Vokeys yet) | ₹10–15k |
| **Putter** | Odyssey White Hot mallet or Ping mallet | ₹10–18k |
| **Bag** | This is what people see first. A clean branded cart bag (Titleist/Ping/Callaway) with matching headcovers | ₹15–25k |

**Skip for now:** 3-wood (hard to hit), 60° lob wedge, blades/players irons of any kind.

**Sample allocation at ₹1.2L:** used premium driver ₹30k + new GI irons ₹60k + one hybrid ₹14k + putter ₹12k + bag ₹18k. Sell/donate the starter set — don't mix it in.

### Where in Bangalore
- **Clubworks** (Bangalore) — premium brands, launch-monitor fitting. Get fitted here before buying anything.
- **GolfEdge India** (online, authorized dealer) — often better prices; buy after your Clubworks fitting tells you specs.
- **Golf Garage** — pre-owned premium (driver, putter).

### The soft gear that matters more than clubs
- Golf shoes (spikeless fine): FootJoy/Ecco, kept clean
- 2 collared polos that fit + tailored shorts/chinos (never denim, never round-neck tees)
- Leather glove (left hand for you), replace when shiny
- Balls: Srixon Q-Star / Callaway Supersoft to play; keep a marked ball routine
- Pitch-mark repair tool + ball marker in pocket **always** — reaching for these unprompted signals you belong more than any driver does

## 2. Know Enough to Not Be an Outsider

### Fitting vocabulary (use these words at the shop)
- **Flex** — shaft stiffness: L/A/R/S/X. Driver carry under ~200m → Regular. Don't ego-buy Stiff.
- **Lie angle** — sole angle at address; wrong lie = pushes/pulls. Fitter checks with tape.
- **Loft & gapping** — even distance gaps between clubs; ask "what's my gapping from PW to sand wedge?"
- **Grip size** — measured off your hand; midsize if fingers don't touch palm.
- **Swing weight / kick point** — know they exist; let the fitter decide.
- Say: *"I'm playing off roughly 28–30, looking for game-improvement irons, regular flex, and I'd like a gapping check."* That sentence alone earns respect.

### Etiquette that gets you invited back (in priority order)
1. **Pace.** Be ready to hit when it's your turn ("ready golf"). Walk briskly. A slow beginner is forgiven; a slow *oblivious* beginner is not. If your group falls a hole behind, wave faster groups through.
2. **Silence & stillness** when anyone addresses the ball. Stand outside their peripheral vision, never behind the hole or their line.
3. **Repair the course:** replace/sand divots, fix your pitch mark *plus one more*, rake bunkers (enter from the low side, rake backwards out).
4. **On the green:** never walk on anyone's putting line. Mark your ball with a coin. Tend or remove the flag when asked. Closest to hole tends.
5. **Order:** honors on the tee (best previous hole goes first); farthest from hole plays first thereafter. When in doubt, ask "ready golf?"
6. **Scorecard:** you keep your marker's score, they keep yours. Announce your score walking off the green ("that's a 7 — no, 8 with the penalty"). Never fudge. A golfer who counts every stroke at 110 is respected; a "95" that was 108 is spotted instantly.
7. **Caddie culture (India-specific):** caddie fee at booking + tip ₹300–500 for 18 holes (more if he reads greens well). Ask his name, use it. How you treat the caddie is watched.
8. **Phone on silent, calls only between holes, never on the tee or green.**

### Rules you must know cold
- Out of bounds / lost ball: stroke *and* distance (re-hit from original spot, +1). Provisional ball: announce "playing a provisional" before hitting.
- Penalty areas (red/yellow stakes): drop within 2 club-lengths of entry point, +1.
- Unplayable lie: 3 options, all +1.
- Max score: for handicap posting, your max on a hole is **net double bogey** — pick up and move on, it keeps pace.
- Preferred lies ("winter rules") — only when the club declares it.

### Lingo cheat-sheet
Up-and-down (chip+putt to save score) · gimme (conceded short putt — casual only, never in comps) · fried egg (plugged bunker ball) · fore! (yell it, loud, no hesitation) · playing off 18 (handicap) · gross vs net · four-ball, foursomes, Stableford, scramble (formats — see the app bytes) · "press" (side-bet double) · dormie · 19th hole (the bar).

## 3. Getting a Real Handicap (the exact path)

1. **Register with IGU WHS as a Non-Club Member**: [whs.indiangolfunion.org](https://whs.indiangolfunion.org/) → ₹2,500/year (+5% gateway +18% GST ≈ ₹3,100). This is official — India's version of iGolf. No club membership needed.
2. **Post 54 holes** (any mix of 9s and 18s) with attested scorecards from rated courses → you get an initial **Handicap Index**. Clover Greens is rated.
3. Every round after: card signed by playing partner, enter score in the IGU WHS portal same day. Index = best 8 of your last 20 differentials, so it self-corrects as you improve.
4. **Target milestones:** Index established (month 2) → under 28 (BMW Golf Cup Category B eligible) → under 20 (most corporate opens) → under 12 (Category A, serious amateur events).

### Tournaments to aim at
- **BMW Golf Cup** — India's biggest amateur event; Bengaluru qualifier; Cat B = handicap 13–28, so you're eligible the moment your index lands. This is the realistic Year-1 goal.
- **4moles.com events** — Bangalore corporate/social tournaments most months; low barrier, great for network-building into the community you want.
- **Credit-card program events** — your card's golf program (Golftripz/ReserveGolf etc.) runs member tournaments; check the app.
- **IGU amateur championships** — later, when index < 12.

## 4. Tee-Time Engine (your constraints: book 14 days out, max 3 live bookings, Wed–Sun 1–5 PM)

### The rolling system
Every **Sunday evening**, book the slot that just opened 14 days out. You'll always hold 3 bookings ≈ **3 rounds per 2 weeks**. Pattern:

- **Booking 1 & 2:** Clover Greens (home base — learn one course deeply; knowing where to miss is worth 10 strokes)
- **Booking 3:** rotate — Zion Hills (beginner-friendly), Eagleton, Prestige Golfshire (pay-per-play), KGA as guest, Bangalore Golf Club

### Timing (Bangalore light)
18 holes ≈ 4.5h at your level. Sunset: ~6:00 PM Nov–Jan, ~6:45 Apr–Jun.
- **Nov–Feb: tee off by 1:00–1:15 PM** or book 9 holes instead
- **Mar–Oct: 1:30 PM is safe**
- Monsoon (Jun–Sep): afternoon storms — take the 1 PM slot, back-load range sessions as rain backup.

### 12-week ramp
| Weeks | Focus |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Get fitted → buy set. 2 range sessions/wk (7-iron + wedge only). Book first 3 tee slots. Register IGU WHS. |
| 3–6 | 3 rounds per 2 wks at Clover Greens. **Post every score.** By week 6: 54+ holes posted → **Handicap Index arrives.** Range once/wk: driver + putting drills. |
| 7–9 | Add one away course per rotation. Play one round with a 4moles event group. Start tracking fairways/GIR/putts per round. |
| 10–12 | Enter first 4moles social tournament (Stableford — forgiving format). Book a playing lesson at Clover Greens: one hour with a pro *on the course*, not the range — fastest respectability gain available. |

**Coaching note:** budget ₹1,500–2,500/session for a coach at Clover Greens or KGA range, fortnightly. Self-taught 110-shooters plateau at 100; coached ones reach low 90s in a year — and low 90s with good etiquette is fully "belongs here" territory.
